Adam Sandler

Fun Facts About a Comedy Legend
  • Adam began his career as a stand-up comedian in 1987, at the age of 17. In his early career, he landed a small role in The Cosby Show, made regular appearances on the MTV game show Remote Control, and starred in his first feature film, Going Overboard.
  • His big break came in 1990, when he was hired as a writer for Saturday Night Live. The following year, he became a cast member. In his five years at SNL, Adam played memorable characters like "Opera Man," "Cajun Man" and "Canteen Boy" and performed original songs, the most famous being "The Chanukah Song."
  • His first major film role came in 1995, with Billy Madison. Since then, he's starred in many hit comedies, including Happy Gilmore, The Waterboy, The Wedding Singer, 50 First Dates, the Grown Ups franchise, Just Go with It, Blended, Murder Mystery, and Hubie Halloween.
  • While best known for live-action comedies, Adam has also received critical praise for his roles in dramatic films like Punch-Drunk Love, Spanglish, Funny People, The Meyerowitz Stories, and Uncut Gems. He also starred in the animated comedy Eight Crazy Nights and the first three films in the Hotel Transylvania franchise.
  • In 1999, Adam founded his own film and television production company, Happy Madison Productions. The company has produced most of Adam's films since then.
  • Adam is known for casting his friends in his movies. Major collaborators include Rob Schneider, David Spade, Kevin James, Steve Buscemi, Chris Rock, John Turturro, Peter Dante, Allen Covert, Jonathan Loughran, Jon Lovitz, and Nick Swardson. His wife Jackie and his daughters, Sadie and Sunny, also appear frequently in his films.
  • To date, Adam has produced six comedy albums. Two were certified double-platinum, and three received Grammy nominations.
  • In 2019, Adam won an Independent Spirit Award for his role in Uncut Gems. He's also won 6 MTV Movie Awards, 11 Nickelodeon Kids' Choice Awards, 9 People's Choice Awards, and 8 Teen Choice Awards over the the course of his career.
  • Adam donates generously to charity. In 2007, he donated $1 million to the Boys and Girls Club in his hometown of Manchester, New Hampshire. He has also participated in fundraising efforts for autism awareness, Hurricane Sandy Relief, The Thirst Project, and a relief fund for stand-up comics struggling during the COVID-19 Pandemic.
